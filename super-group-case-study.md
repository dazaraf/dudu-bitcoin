### 2. Chain Selection

This is where most stablecoin projects make their first critical mistake — choosing a chain based on hype rather than fit.

I evaluated multiple options including Solana, EVM-compatible chains, XRPL, and Canton Network against Super Group's specific requirements:

- **Transaction throughput**: Gaming and betting generate high-frequency, low-value transactions. The chain needed to handle volume without prohibitive gas fees.
- **User ecosystem**: Where are crypto-native users in the gaming and betting space already active?
- **Developer tooling and infrastructure**: What would the integration lift look like for Super Group's engineering team?
- **Stablecoin transaction activity**: Raw market cap doesn't tell the full story. Solana holds only ~4% of the $306B stablecoin market by capitalization, but its share of actual stablecoin transaction volume is significantly higher.

The recommendation: **Solana** — the best fit for Super Group's transaction profile, user base, and speed-to-market.

## Key Takeaways

**Stablecoin strategy is a distribution problem, not a technology problem.** The smart contracts are largely commoditized. What separates successful stablecoins from dead ones is whether the issuer has the user base, merchant relationships, and go-to-market discipline to drive real adoption.

**Companies with large captive audiences are best positioned to launch branded stablecoins** — but only if they pair that audience with seamless on/off ramps and merchant acceptance from day one.

**Chain selection should be driven by transaction profile, not market cap.** For Super Group's high-frequency, low-value use case, Solana's throughput and cost structure made it the clear choice over higher-cap alternatives.
