📊 Lead with Data

Adding statistics is the #1 tactic.
+41% visibility boost.

  • Specific numbers beat vague claims every time
  • Attribute every stat to a credible source
  • Publish original research — competitors can't duplicate it
  • Use tables for data — AI extracts structured data more easily
Pro tip: "47% growth in Q1 2026" gets cited. "Significant growth" gets filtered out. Every claim needs a number attached.
Common mistake: Hedging language and ranges. AI pulls high-confidence assertions — be specific or be invisible.

🏗️ BLUF Structure

Answer the question in the first 200 words. Don't build up to it.

  • AI evaluates opening content most heavily
  • Write 40-60 word self-contained answer blocks
  • Each section must stand alone as a passage
  • Direct answer first, then supporting context
Pro tip: Write every section like a featured snippet. Both Google and AI engines reward the same format: answer first, context second.
Common mistake: Long intros, "In this article we'll explore..." — throat-clearing paragraphs. The AI already stopped reading.

❓ FAQ Schema

Pages with FAQ markup get cited 3.2x more by AI engines.

  • Use real questions your audience actually asks
  • Implement FAQPage schema in JSON-LD format
  • Match how people naturally query AI assistants
  • 41% citation rate vs 15% without — 2.7x higher
Pro tip: Ask ChatGPT "what do people ask about [your topic]?" Then answer every one on your page with schema markup.
Common mistake: Writing FAQs nobody searches for. Use Search Console, Reddit and forums for real questions — not the ones you wish people asked.

🎯 Know Each AI

Every AI engine has different source preferences. One size won't fit.

  • ChatGPT: Wikipedia dominates (47.9% of top-10 citations)
  • Perplexity: Reddit is king (46.7% of top-10 citations)
  • Google AI Overviews: most balanced — Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn
  • Claude: highest weight on research sources and accreditations
Pro tip: For ChatGPT: get a Wikipedia mention. Perplexity: be active on Reddit. Google: diversify across YouTube, LinkedIn, and your site.
Common mistake: Treating "AI search" as one monolith. ChatGPT and Perplexity have completely different source preferences. Know your audience's platform.

🕸️ Topic Clusters

Sites with 5+ interlinked pages get 3.2x more AI citations.

  • Build pillar pages + 8-15 deep-dive cluster articles
  • Interlink everything with descriptive anchor text
  • 86% of AI citations come from topic-clustered sites
  • General queries → pillar cited. Specific → cluster cited
Pro tip: Pick 3-5 pillar topics you want to own. Build depth, not breadth. AI recognizes expertise when you cover every angle.
Common mistake: 50 thin posts on 50 topics. AI sees you as expert in nothing. Be the definitive source on 3 topics rather than mediocre on 50.

🔑 Entity Authority

Not in the Knowledge Graph? Content optimization alone won't save you.

  • Consistent brand info across every platform
  • Schema.org Organization + Person markup is the minimum
  • Third-party mentions outweigh owned content for AI trust
  • Google's Knowledge Graph: 800B facts about 8B entities
Pro tip: Get your brand into the Knowledge Graph as a distinct, verifiable entity. Focus on attributes (what you ARE) not just what you do.
Common mistake: Different names, bios, descriptions across platforms. AI cross-references everything — inconsistency = low confidence = no citation.

📎 Cite Sources

Citing sources in your own content boosts your visibility 30-40%.

  • AI treats well-cited content as more trustworthy
  • Include expert quotes with clear attribution
  • Link to authoritative external sources
  • "According to [Source], [claim]" pattern works best
Pro tip: AI recognizes attribution patterns. Content that backs up its claims with named sources gets rewarded — you cite others, AI cites you.
Common mistake: Opinion pieces with zero external validation. If you're the only one saying it, AI treats it as low-confidence and skips you.

🤖 llms.txt File

Create /llms.txt — the robots.txt for AI crawlers.

  • A markdown map of your key pages, written for LLMs
  • Allow GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot in robots.txt
  • Create /llms.txt (overview) + /llms-full.txt (full content)
  • Early adopters get indexed before competitors know it exists
Pro tip: Think of llms.txt as your elevator pitch to every AI on the internet. Curate it — only include your strongest, most authoritative pages.
Common mistake: Blocking AI crawlers to "protect content." You become invisible to every AI search engine. That's the real cost.

Top Mistakes That Kill
Your AI Visibility

Keyword stuffing

Underperforms baseline by 10% on Perplexity. LLMs detect and penalize forced phrasing.

No schema markup

Leaving a 2.7-3.2x citation boost on the table. FAQ + Article schema is the bare minimum.

Ignoring off-site signals

AI cross-references sources. Zero third-party mentions = zero credibility to the model.

Stale content

Visibility drops after 3-6 months. AI citations are 25.7% fresher than Google organic results.

No measurement

Test 20-50 prompts monthly across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI. Track what cites you.

Treating GEO as just SEO

Entity optimization, citation-building, structured passages — it's a different playbook entirely.
