01
📊 Lead with Data
+41%

The Princeton study found that adding statistics is the single highest-performing GEO tactic.

  • Concrete numbers outperform qualitative claims — "revenue grew 34% in Q1" beats "revenue grew significantly" every time
  • Always attribute data to a named, credible source — AI engines cross-check and reward verifiable claims
  • First-party research is the ultimate moat — original data can't be duplicated by competitors
Pro tip: Use tables when presenting data. AI models extract structured formats far more reliably than numbers buried in paragraphs. Think: comparison tables, benchmark charts, ranked lists.
Common mistake: Hedging with "approximately" or "around 30-50%." AI models pull high-confidence assertions for synthesis — vague ranges get filtered out as low-quality signal.

02
🏗️ BLUF Structure
First 200
Words

AI retrieval systems judge your page primarily on its opening content. Lead with the answer.

  • The first 200 words should directly and completely answer the primary query — don't build up to it with background context
  • Structure content as 40-60 word "answer blocks" that each stand alone — this is how AI extracts passages for responses
  • Use clean H2/H3 hierarchies so AI can parse your page into retrievable, self-contained sections
Pro tip: Featured snippets and AI answers share the same preference: paragraph snippets of ~45 words. Write each section opener as if it will be quoted standalone — because it probably will be.
Common mistake: Throat-clearing intros like "In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore..." AI models evaluate the first 200 words most heavily. If the answer isn't there, the page gets skipped entirely.

03
❓ FAQ Schema
3.2x

Pages with FAQ markup achieve a 41% citation rate — vs just 15% without it.

  • AI engines are built for Q&A — FAQ sections on your page get pulled directly into responses when users ask matching questions
  • Implement FAQPage schema in JSON-LD format, using real questions sourced from Search Console, Reddit threads, and community forums
  • Format each FAQ as: question in H3 header, then a concise 40-60 word answer paragraph underneath — this is exactly how AI extracts them
Pro tip: Ask ChatGPT "what questions do people ask about [your topic]?" Use the output as your FAQ blueprint. Answer many related questions on one page to maximize your chances of matching the AI's query.
Common mistake: Writing FAQs you think are important rather than ones people actually search. The disconnect between what you want to answer and what people actually ask is where visibility dies.

🎯 Know Each AI
680M
Citations

An analysis of 680M citations reveals each AI engine has wildly different source preferences.

  • ChatGPT favors Wikipedia (47.9% of top-10 share) and established authoritative knowledge bases like Forbes, Statista, and McKinsey
  • Perplexity prioritizes community content — Reddit alone accounts for 46.7% of its top-10 citations, followed by YouTube and Gartner
  • Google AI Overviews is the most balanced — it distributes citations across Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, Quora, and traditional media
Pro tip: Map which AI platforms your audience uses most, then optimize for that platform's source preferences first. A Wikipedia mention is gold for ChatGPT; Reddit authority is gold for Perplexity.
Common mistake: Building one "AI-optimized" content strategy for all platforms. The platforms are fundamentally different in what they trust — a one-size approach means you're optimized for none of them.

05
🕸️ Topic Clusters
86%

86% of AI citations come from websites with 5+ interconnected pages on the same topic.

  • Build pillar pages (2-3K words, comprehensive overview) linked to 8-15 cluster articles that each deep-dive into a specific subtopic
  • Interlink everything with descriptive anchor text — AI systems recognize topical authority when you cover a subject from every angle
  • For broad queries, your pillar page gets cited. For specific queries, your cluster articles get cited. You win either way.
Pro tip: Pick 3-5 pillar topics you want to own completely. Depth beats breadth every time — AI models recognize genuine domain expertise when you cover every angle of a subject comprehensively.
Common mistake: Publishing 50 thin posts across 50 unrelated topics. The AI sees you as an authority on nothing. It's better to be the definitive source on 3 topics than a mediocre voice on 50.

06
🔑 Entity Authority
800B Facts

Google's Knowledge Graph holds 800B facts about 8B entities. If your brand isn't one of them, content alone won't get you cited.

  • Maintain perfectly consistent brand name, description, and details across every platform — LinkedIn, Crunchbase, directories, your site, social bios
  • Implement Schema.org Organization + Person markup as the bare minimum — this is how AI engines identify and verify your brand as a real entity
  • Third-party mentions drive AI citations more than your own content — earned media, guest posts, directory listings, and reviews all count
Pro tip: Think of your brand as a node in a massive knowledge graph. The more attributes (what you are), relationships (who you're connected to), and verifiable facts you have, the more confidently AI will cite you.
Common mistake: Using different brand names, bios, or descriptions across platforms. AI cross-references everything it finds — inconsistency signals low confidence, and low confidence means no citation.

07
📎 Cite Sources
+40%

The Princeton study found that adding citations to your own content increases YOUR visibility by 30-40%.

  • AI engines treat well-cited content as inherently more trustworthy — when you reference credible sources, the AI extends that credibility to you
  • Include expert quotes with clear attribution using the "According to [Source], [claim]" format — AI recognizes and rewards this pattern
  • It's a virtuous cycle: you cite authoritative sources → AI trusts your content more → AI cites you in its responses → you become an authoritative source
Pro tip: Don't just link out — actively reference and name your sources inline. "According to McKinsey's 2026 AI report, agent spending will reach $X" is far more citable than a bare hyperlink at the end.
Common mistake: Publishing opinion pieces and hot takes with zero external validation. If no other credible source backs up your claim, AI treats it as low-confidence speculation and skips you entirely.

08
🤖 llms.txt File
/llms.txt

A new standard is emerging: a markdown file at your site root that tells AI crawlers exactly what matters.

  • Create /llms.txt as a curated map of your most important pages — think of it as your elevator pitch to every AI engine on the internet
  • Make sure GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot are allowed in your robots.txt — blocking them means total invisibility in AI search results
  • Go further with /llms-full.txt that includes complete content for each key page — early adopters are getting indexed before competitors even know this exists
Pro tip: Be selective with what goes in llms.txt. Only feature your strongest, most authoritative pages — it's a curated pitch, not a sitemap. Quality of signal matters more than quantity of pages.
Common mistake: Blocking AI crawlers because "they're scraping my content for free." The trade-off is real: you become completely invisible to every AI-powered search engine. For most brands, that's a far greater cost than the training data.

Top Mistakes That
Kill Your AI Visibility

Keyword stuffing

Actually underperforms baseline by 10% on Perplexity. LLMs detect forced phrasing and penalize it — write like a human expert, not a bot.

No schema markup

You're leaving a 2.7-3.2x citation boost on the table. FAQ + Article schema in JSON-LD is the absolute bare minimum for AI visibility.

Ignoring off-site signals

AI cross-references multiple sources before citing. A website claiming expertise with zero third-party validation gets treated as unverified.

Stale content

Content loses AI visibility after 3-6 months without updates. AI citations are 25.7% fresher than Google organic — recency is a ranking signal.

No measurement

Test 20-50 buyer-intent prompts monthly across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI. If you're not tracking citations, you can't improve them.

Treating GEO as just SEO

GEO requires entity optimization, citation-building, structured passages, and off-site authority management — it's a fundamentally different playbook.
